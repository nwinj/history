*Who were the early nationalists.

A group of leaders from the educated middle-class who believed and open-minded and moderate politics. They asked for constitutional and other reforms within the framework of British rule as they had faith in the British sense of justice and fair play

*What were the beliefs of the early nationalists.

1.they had faith in the sense of justice, Fairplay, honesty and integrity of the British.

2. They hoped to be granted home rule.

3.they believed that the British rule aided in cleansing social ills like sati, untouchability, and Child marriage.

4.They thought that the British would help Indian govern them selves according to Western standards.

5. The main obstacle to India's progress was social and economic backwardness and not the British colonial rule.

6.they relied on constitutional and peaceful methods to achieve their aims


*What were the constitutional reforms demanded by the early nationalists.

1.abolition of the India Council.

2.expansion of the legislative Council and legislative assemblies both at the Centre and in the provinces.

3.in the beginning of the 20th century they demanded self government like in Canada and Australia.

4.adequate representation of Indians in the executive council of the viceroy and of the governors.

*What were the economic reforms demanded by the early nationalists.

1.reduction in expenditure on the army and the money does saved was to be spent on welfare activities such as health and education.

2.reduction in land revenue and protection of peasants against the Zamindars.

3.availability of cheap credit to the peasants through agricultural banks.

4.development of banking, irrigation, medical and health facilities.

5. abolition of the salt tax and the duty on sugar.
